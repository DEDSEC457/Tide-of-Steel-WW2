HOW TO PLAY

Goal. Germany must capture the Soviet objective cities (Moscow is worth the most) before the campaign ends on turn 28 (early January 1942). The Soviets win by holding them. Each turn is one week of 1941.

  • Select one of your units, then click a highlighted hex to move, or click an adjacent enemy (red ring) to attack. Every unit can move and attack once per turn. You can drive deep into enemy ground — but any hex next to an enemy unit halts movement for the turn (zone of control). Punch a hole in their line, then pour through it.
  • Strength (the number on the counter) is a unit's health and its punch. Spend ⚙ Production to reinforce a damaged unit or deploy new ones at your cities.
  • Terrain matters. Forests, hills and cities boost defense. Rivers are deadly to attack across. Units that hold still dig in (▮ pips = bonus defense). The map is tinted with the front line: grey ground is Axis-held, red is Soviet — watch it shift as the armies advance.
  • SUPPLY is everything. Supply reaches only 7 hexes from your map edge (Germans west, Soviets east) — and from every city you control, which acts as a forward depot. Capture cities to push your supply network deeper. A unit cut off (by distance, or because enemies stand between it and home) shows ⚠ OUT OF SUPPLY and a red ring: it attacks at 40%, defends at 65%, cannot dig in or reinforce, and starves — losing strength every single turn. Surround enemy armies to destroy them — and don't let it happen to your spearheads.
  • The clock. October brings mud (slow, weak attacks). December brings snow — German attacks falter while fresh Siberian divisions arrive for the Soviets. Germany: win before winter. Soviets: survive until it.
  • A unit forced to retreat with nowhere to go is destroyed — pockets kill.
  • ⭐ Generals. Historical commanders lead their formations — Guderian's panzers hit harder and drive deeper, Rokossovsky's riflemen are hard to dislodge. Units with a general show a gold star; the unit panel and battle forecast show the effect. A general is lost with his unit. From early October, Zhukov takes over the Moscow defense: Soviet units near the capital defend better while it holds.
  • 📰 History unfolds. Real dispatches from 1941 appear as the weeks pass — a few carry production bonuses. In mid-August the German side faces the winter question: divert 10 ⚙ to winter equipment, or gamble that the war ends first. Gear mostly saves the defense in deep snow and keeps the infantry marching — panzers freeze and attacks stay hard either way.
  • ✈ Air forces (side panel): each air group can Strike an enemy unit — damage plus blasting away its dig-in — within 8 hexes of a city you control (airfields), or fly Patrol to intercept enemy strikes. Groups that stay grounded repair. Flak and dogfights cause losses — and deep snow all but grounds the Luftwaffe.

Tips: punch holes with panzers, pour through, and meet behind the enemy line — that's an encirclement. As the Soviets, keep a straight front, garrison cities, and always have an escape route east.

THE WINTER QUESTION

QUARTERMASTER-GENERAL'S REPORT · MID-AUGUST 1941

The army has winter clothing for barely one soldier in five. The railways can haul ammunition and fuel for the offensive — or greatcoats, felt boots and stoves for three million men. The General Staff is confident: Russia will collapse before the first snow.

If they are wrong, and the army is still in the field in December: without winter equipment the men can barely hold their positions, let alone march. With it, the infantry keeps moving and the line holds far better — though no greatcoat will unfreeze a panzer's engine, or make a winter offensive easy.

Short on production? The order can still be placed on credit — your balance goes negative, and future income pays it off (you can't reinforce or deploy until you're out of debt).
