HOW TO PLAY

Goal. Germany must capture the Soviet objective cities (Moscow is worth the most) before the campaign ends on turn 28 (early January 1942). The Soviets win by holding them. Each turn is one week of 1941.

  • Select one of your units, then click a highlighted hex to move, or click an adjacent enemy (red ring) to attack. Every unit can move and attack once per turn. You can drive deep into enemy ground — but any hex next to an enemy unit halts movement for the turn (zone of control). Punch a hole in their line, then pour through it.
  • Strength (the number on the counter) is a unit's health and its punch. Spend ⚙ Production to reinforce a damaged unit or deploy new ones at your cities.
  • Read the forecast. Hover an enemy you can hit and the Battle Forecast shows the odds, expected losses, and a WHY breakdown of every modifier — terrain, rivers, dig-in, generals, supply, and ganging up — so you always know what's deciding the fight.
  • ⚑ Decisions. At the great historical crossroads you'll be handed a real choice — the Kiev Turn, Nagumo's rearming gamble at Midway, the Mortain counterattack at Normandy — each with lasting consequences. There's no taking it back.
  • Terrain matters. Forests, hills and cities boost defense. Rivers are deadly to attack across. Units that hold still dig in (▮ pips = bonus defense). The map is tinted with the front line: grey ground is Axis-held, red is Soviet — watch it shift as the armies advance.
  • SUPPLY is everything. Supply reaches only 7 hexes from your map edge (Germans west, Soviets east) — and from every city you control, which acts as a forward depot. Capture cities to push your supply network deeper. A unit cut off (by distance, or because enemies stand between it and home) shows ⚠ OUT OF SUPPLY and a red ring: it attacks at 40%, defends at 65%, cannot dig in or reinforce, and starves — losing strength every single turn. Surround enemy armies to destroy them — and don't let it happen to your spearheads.
  • The clock. October brings mud (slow, weak attacks). December brings snow — German attacks falter while fresh Siberian divisions arrive for the Soviets. Germany: win before winter. Soviets: survive until it.
  • A unit forced to retreat with nowhere to go is destroyed — pockets kill.
  • ⭐ Generals & traits. Historical commanders lead their formations, each with a named trait: ⚙ Panzer Leader (Guderian, Rommel…) drives a mechanised formation +1 hex in clear weather; 🛡 Master of Defence (Model, Chuikov) is an immovable anvil; 🌀 The Cauldron (von Manstein) turns encirclements deadly; 🚚 Iron Logistician keeps the guns firing when cut off. Units with a general show a gold star; the unit panel, roster and battle forecast show the effect. A general is lost with his unit — protect them. From October, Zhukov takes the Moscow defense.
  • 🎖 Doctrines & honours. Press D to spend production on permanent war doctrines — the same points that field divisions, so each is a division delayed. Choose an offensive, defensive or logistics path to shape how your army fights. Your divisions also keep a ☠ confirmed-kill tally; the after-action report crowns your top killers.
  • 🎖 Linked campaigns. Fight the whole war front by front — as the ⬛ Axis (Poland → the Ardennes), the 🟥 Soviets (Barbarossa → Kursk), or the 🟦 United States across the Pacific (Midway → Guadalcanal → Leyte Gulf → Okinawa). Win a front to unlock the next; your doctrines, veterans and a war chest carry forward. Quit and resume any battle where you left it.
  • ⚓ The Pacific. Ships fight only at sea, infantry only ashore — invasion convoys are the bridge between them. Carrier decks are tinderboxes: air strikes hit them for double, so in a carrier duel the fleet that strikes first usually wins. Guard your flattops with fighter patrols, and kill theirs on the deck.
  • ✚ Experience. Units harden as they fight — Veteran, Hardened, Elite (gold chevrons on the counter) for +5/+10/+15% to attack and defense. Veterancy survives reinforcement, so keep your best formations alive.
  • ⚔ Combined arms. Shift-click several of your units, then click one adjacent enemy to assault it together — far more punch than attacking one at a time, and a tank + infantry mix lands a bonus on top. If the enemy breaks, you choose which unit advances into the empty hex.
  • ⌂ Headquarters. Each side fields HQs (a command pennant). Friendly units within 5 hexes of an HQ get +5% attack/defense and +1 movement. HQs can't take ground and are weak in a fight — keep them just behind the line, where their command reaches the front.
  • 📰 History unfolds. Real dispatches from 1941 appear as the weeks pass — a few carry production bonuses. In mid-August the German side faces the winter question: divert 10 ⚙ to winter equipment, or gamble that the war ends first. Gear mostly saves the defense in deep snow and keeps the infantry marching — panzers freeze and attacks stay hard either way.
  • Quality of life. U / the ↩ Undo button takes back your last move (until anything else happens). S / the 🚚 Supply button shades every hex outside your supply network — see where your spearheads go dry before they do. With a unit selected, hover any reachable hex to see the exact route it will march and the movement-point price. T / the ⚠ Threat button shades every hex the enemy could strike next turn — deep red where several attackers can converge, so you can see a kill-sack before you step into it. Standing orders (in the unit panel) are set-and-forget battle plans: ⚔ Advance makes a division push toward the nearest enemy objective every turn, 🛡 Hold line marches it to the front and digs it in — both run automatically at the start of each turn (a ► or ⊣ badge marks ordered units), so you command sectors and only micromanage the decisive point. ⏭ Skip leaves a unit in place for one turn without it nagging. Encirclements show on the map: an enemy division you've sealed off with no way back to its supply gets a red dashed ring (it's doomed — hold the pocket and it starves), and one of your own divisions cut off with no escape gets a pulsing amber ring and a turn-start warning — break it out before it's lost. The minimap (bottom-left) shows the whole front — click or drag it to fly the camera anywhere; M hides it. Click the weather chip for the campaign clock. AI: ▶ cycles enemy turn speed (normal / fast / instant). Esc opens the pause menu — quick-save, jump to How-to-Play, or save & quit to the main menu (a first Esc just clears your current selection). Ending your turn with units still ready asks for a second click, and so does overwriting a saved campaign.
  • 🎲 Every campaign is different & your service record. Four systems are always on, so no two wars play the same: Chaos weather re-rolls the season script (watch the campaign clock — it becomes this war's forecast), Shuffled reserves moves every reinforcement up to two weeks early or late, Veteran cadres hands each side three battle-tested formations, and Fog of war hides every enemy your troops haven't spotted: units see 2 hexes (cavalry and carriers 3), cities you hold report around themselves, and air groups can fly 🔭 photo-recon over anywhere your airfields cover. Enemies you lose track of leave a faded ? last-seen marker, and marching into an unseen enemy's path halts your unit on contact — probe with cavalry before you commit the panzers. Every finished game against the computer goes into your service record, shown on the menu: campaigns fought and your best result on each side. Beat your own history.
  • Controls & hotkeys. An amber pip marks every unit that still has orders to give. N / Space / Tab (or the ⏭ Next button) jumps to the next one and centres it; the button also shows how many are left. Arrow keys pan, + / − (and the scroll wheel) zoom, C recentres on your selected unit, and F re-fits the whole map. Right-click cancels a deploy, an air strike or a selection; double-click recentres the map. E ends the turn, H opens this help, Esc clears everything. A ⭐ gold star on a counter marks a formation led by a general. Each new turn a banner gives the date, the weather, and — against the computer — what the enemy did while you were away.
  • ✈ Air forces (side panel): each air group can Strike an enemy unit — damage plus blasting away its dig-in — within 8 hexes of a city you control (airfields) or of a friendly aircraft carrier in naval scenarios, or fly Patrol to intercept enemy strikes. Groups that stay grounded repair. Flak and dogfights cause losses — and deep snow all but grounds an unprepared air force.

Tips: punch holes with panzers, pour through, and meet behind the enemy line — that's an encirclement. As the Soviets, keep a straight front, garrison cities, and always have an escape route east.

THE WINTER QUESTION

QUARTERMASTER-GENERAL'S REPORT · MID-AUGUST 1941

The army has winter clothing for barely one soldier in five. The railways can haul ammunition and fuel for the offensive — or greatcoats, felt boots and stoves for three million men. The General Staff is confident: Russia will collapse before the first snow.

If they are wrong, and the army is still in the field in December: without winter equipment the men can barely hold their positions, let alone march. With it, the infantry keeps moving and the line holds far better — though no greatcoat will unfreeze a panzer's engine, or make a winter offensive easy.

Short on production? The order can still be placed on credit — your balance goes negative, and future income pays it off (you can't reinforce or deploy until you're out of debt).
